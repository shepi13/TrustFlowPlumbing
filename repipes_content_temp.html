Re-Pipes (Partial or Whole House)

All plumbing systems age and will eventually need to be replaced. Common causes that require a re-pipe include corroded copper or galvanized piping, brittle CPVC, or worn-out fittings. Instead of chipping up the home’s foundation for a localized repair, it is often more cost and time-effective to replace all the water piping with state-of-the-art Pex-A Uponor piping. This piping would be installed in the attic as opposed to underground and would only require minimal drywall removal for plumbing access. (Re-pipes do not include any drywall repair). All whole house re-pipes require a permit and inspection, to ensure that your home’s potable water system has been brought up to national code requirements.

Re-pipes include:

  • All Piping Replaced with New Uponor Pex-A (25-Year Manufacturer’s Warranty)
  • New Outdoor Main Water Service
  • New Outdoor Hose Bibs with Vacuum Breakers
  • New Local Shut-Off Valves & Water Supply Lines at Every Fixture
  • New Recessed Ice Line to Refrigerator (Where Applicable)
  • New Recessed Washing Machine Outlet Box (Where Applicable)
  • All Outdoor Pipes Will be Painted (Can Color Match)
  • Existing Shower Valves Will Be Tied into the New Uponor Pex-A System (This is the only tiny section of original piping that will be left until the valve is replaced)
  • Optional Upgrades That Can be Installed During the Re-pipe at a Reduced Rate if Needed or Desired (Must be disclosed ahead of time):
    • New Shower Valves
    • New Water Heaters
    • New Faucets
